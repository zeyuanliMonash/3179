Seismic Explorer: Visual Analysis of Global Earthquake Data from 2000-2022

Global Earthquake Distribution Map
This interactive map displays the distribution of earthquakes around the world. Each circle on the map represents an earthquake event, with its color indicating the earthquake's magnitude. The map also highlights the top five deepest earthquakes, providing insights into their magnitude, depth, and location.
Count of Earthquakes for Different Magnitudes
Engage with the data through this bar chart that showcases the count of earthquakes for different magnitude intervals. The chart provides an interactive experience, allowing you to filter the data displayed based on a selected minimum magnitude. The color-coded bars represent different magnitude intervals, making it easy to identify and analyze the distribution and frequency of earthquake occurrences of varying strengths. For detailed counts for each interval, just simply hover over the bars.
Magnitude and Timing of Worldwide Earthquakes
"Visualize the timing and magnitude of earthquakes across the globe. Each point on the chart represents an earthquake event, with its position indicating the time of occurrence and magnitude. Filter options are available to focus on earthquakes above a certain magnitude."
Earthquake Occurrences Over Years with Monthly Details stackchart
"Explore the number of earthquakes occurring each year, detailed by months, through this color-coded bar chart. The chart provides an intuitive understanding of earthquake patterns over time, offering insights into monthly variations within each year."
Number of Earthquakes in Oceanic vs Non-Oceanic Regions
This visually appealing pie chart showcases two distinct areas where earthquakes occur: Ocean and Non-Ocean. The blue segment represents the number of earthquakes in Ocean areas, while the green segment represents the number of earthquakes in Non-Ocean areas.
Visual Overview of Earthquake Frequency and Magnitude Per Quarter
This area chart presents a count of earthquakes occurring each quarter, color-coded by the magnitude of earthquakes. The visualization provides an intuitive display of both the frequency and intensity of earthquakes over time. Select a time range in the lower chart to view details for specific quarters in the upper chart.
